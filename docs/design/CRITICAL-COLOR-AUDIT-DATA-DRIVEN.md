## PART 2: NEUROSCIENCE OF COLOR IN SPORTS PERFORMANCE

### üß† What Science Says About Color & Athletic Performance

**RED:**
- ‚úÖ Increases heart rate and adrenaline
- ‚úÖ Improved forceful and explosive motor performance
- ‚úÖ High arousal (Yerkes-Dodson law)
- ‚ùå Impaired intellectual performance and endurance
- ‚ùå Stimulates "avoidance motivation" (defensive purposes)
- **Verdict:** Good for explosive power, BAD for endurance

**BLUE:**
- ‚úÖ Improves focus and concentration
- ‚úÖ Calming effect, reduces stress
- ‚úÖ Better for strategic thinking and precision
- ‚úÖ Induces "approach motivation" and creativity
- ‚úÖ Good for endurance sports
- **Verdict:** Excellent for endurance and focus

**GREEN:**
- ‚úÖ Represents balance and harmony
- ‚úÖ Reduces fatigue
- ‚úÖ Mental clarity and stability
- ‚úÖ Calms anxiety while maintaining energy
- ‚ö†Ô∏è Effects are "modest" (2022 meta-analysis of 69 studies)
- **Verdict:** Balanced, but scientifically weak evidence

**Critical Caveat:**
> "A 2022 meta-analysis found that credible evidence for color impacting physical performance is pretty thin. Effects are modest compared to actual skill level."

**Sources:**
- [Red vs Blue Motor Tasks (Frontiers Neuroscience)](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/psychology/articles/10.3389/fpsyg.2021.744913/full)
- [Color in Sport Meta-Analysis](https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/1612197X.2022.2138497)
- [Color Psychology in Sportswear](https://tdsportswear.com/blog/color-psychology-in-sportswear-how-your-outfit-affects-your-game/)
