## 1. What is HYROX?

### 1.1 Competition Format

HYROX is a global indoor fitness racing competition that combines running with functional workout stations. The format is standardized across all events worldwide, enabling global leaderboards and rankings.

**Core Structure:**
- 8 x 1km running segments (8km total running distance)
- 8 functional workout stations between each run
- Always performed indoors in exhibition halls
- Same format globally for consistent comparison

**Race Flow:**
Run 1km ‚Üí Station 1 ‚Üí Run 1km ‚Üí Station 2 ‚Üí Run 1km ‚Üí Station 3... (repeat 8 times)

### 1.2 The 8 Workout Stations

1. **SkiErg** - 1,000m
   - Targets: Arms, shoulders, core, lower body (when done efficiently)
   - Predominantly upper body focused

2. **Sled Push** - 50m (25m down + 25m back)
   - Targets: Lower body, posterior chain, core, anterior thigh
   - Weight includes the sled itself
   - Note: Much harder on HYROX carpet than gym floors

3. **Sled Pull** - 50m (25m down + 25m back)
   - Targets: Glutes, back, biceps, entire trunk
   - Weight includes the sled

4. **Burpee Broad Jumps** - 80m
   - Bodyweight exercise (no added weight)
   - Combines burpees with forward jumps

5. **Rowing** - 1,000m
   - Marks the beginning of second half
   - Full-body cardio endurance

6. **Farmers Carry** - 200m
   - Targets: Upper back, core, grip strength
   - Longest carry distance

7. **Sandbag Lunges** - 100m
   - Targets: Legs, core, balance
   - High metabolic demand

8. **Wall Balls** - 75-100 reps (division dependent)
   - Targets: Full body, shoulders, legs
   - Different rep counts and weights by division

### 1.3 Weight Standards by Division (2024/25 & 2025/26 Seasons)

**Division Structure:**
- **Open Division**: Lighter weights for newcomers and recreational athletes
- **Pro Division**: Heavier weights for advanced/competitive athletes
- **Doubles Division**: Two-person teams sharing workload
- **Mixed Doubles**: Uses Men's Open weights for both partners (exception: Wall Balls)
- **Relay Division**: Four-person teams, each completing 2 runs + 2 stations
- **Adaptive Division**: Inclusive category with adjustments for permanent impairments

**Key Weight Notes:**
- Mixed Doubles use Men's Open weights for sleds
- Wall Balls in Mixed Doubles: Men throw to 3.00m target, Women to 2.70m target
- World Championships: All competitors use Pro weights (except Mixed Doubles and 60+ age groups)
- Pro Doubles available for Men and Women only (no Mixed Pro Doubles)
- Starting 2025/26: Pro Doubles age groups stop at 59

**Age Groups:**
- Must be 16+ to participate (any division)
- Pro divisions: Age groups stop at 59
- Open divisions: No upper age limit
- Further divided by age brackets for fair competition

### 1.4 Competition Rules

**Key Requirements:**
- All workouts must be completed in correct order
- Movements must meet specified standards
- Correct weights must be used for your division
- Correct reps/distances must be completed
- No time limit to complete the race
- No headphones allowed during competition
- Water/gels only if carried from start (aid stations provided)
- Cannot accept items from spectators (except at aid stations)

## 4. Market Gaps & Opportunities

### 4.1 Key User Pain Points & Complaints

#### **Customer Service & Policies (HYROX Official)**
From TrustPilot and user reviews:

1. **No Refunds/Transfers**
   - "HYROX refuses to allow name changes or substitutions on tickets unless an additional 'Flex' fee was paid"
   - "Hyrox does not offer refunds, so the community is overflowing with scammers"
   - Injury scenario: "Asked to transfer due to injury, told all races sold out for year (in January), appalled by lack of care"
   - Solution introduced 2025/26: Flex Tickets (but must be purchased upfront)

2. **Non-Existent Customer Support**
   - "Customer support is non-existent, emails going unanswered, no clear complaints process"
   - Merchandise issues: "Ordered shoes, received wrong size twice, company stopped responding"
   - Volunteer payment: "Volunteers judged for 30 hours, never received payment, emails ignored"

3. **Race Access Issues**
   - High demand leading to lottery systems (London 2024: 15,000+ racers)
   - Ticket resale problems (now addressed with official-only sales)
   - Rapid sellouts create FOMO and frustration

#### **Training & Preparation Challenges**

From research on athlete struggles:

1. **Injuries & Overuse**
   - Top 3 areas: Achilles, patella (knee), rotator cuff (shoulder)
   - Other vulnerable: Ankles, lower back
   - Cause: "Significantly increased exposure to high force/velocity training"
   - Insufficient recovery between sessions

2. **Mobility Limitations**
   - Ankles: Limited dorsiflexion reduces stride and sled push power
   - Hips: Tight/weak rotators affect lunges and burpees
   - Not enough mobility work in training

3. **Training Balance Confusion**
   - "Fairly well understood how to train for running... strength... but combining? That's difficult"
   - Athletes train too intensely every day
   - "Training at consistently high intensity is tough to recover from, doesn't build aerobic fitness"
   - Lack of structured periodization
